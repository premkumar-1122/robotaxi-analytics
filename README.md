# US Robotaxi Safety Intelligence & Incident Analytics

## Project Objective
This project analyzes publicly available autonomous vehicle safety and incident data from major US robotaxi companies to identify operational risk patterns, safety trends, and deployment challenges across different cities and driving conditions.

## Business Problem
As autonomous ride-hailing services expand across the United States, safety performance has become one of the most critical factors affecting public trust, regulatory approval, and commercial scalability. Companies operating robotaxi fleets must continuously monitor incidents, disengagement events, environmental conditions, and operational risks to improve system reliability and reduce safety concerns.

## Project Goals
- Analyze autonomous vehicle incident trends over time
- Compare safety performance across robotaxi companies
- Identify geographic and environmental risk patterns
- Study relationships between operating conditions and incidents
- Generate insights that could support operational decision-making and safety improvements

## Key Questions
1. Which robotaxi companies report the highest number of incidents?
2. Are safety incidents increasing or decreasing over time?
3. Which environmental or geographic conditions appear most risky?
4. Which cities show safer operating conditions for robotaxi deployment?
5. What operational insights can be derived from the available data?

## Expected Outcomes
The project aims to build an end-to-end analytics workflow involving data collection, cleaning, SQL analysis, visualization, and dashboard development to generate actionable insights from autonomous vehicle safety data.
